'''Charles Biddle''' (December 24, 1745 – April 4, 1821) was a [[Pennsylvania]] statesman and a member of the prominent [[Biddle family]] of [[Philadelphia, Pennsylvania]].

==Early life==
Biddle was born to a wealthy old [[Quaker]] family on December 24, 1745 in [[Philadelphia, Pennsylvania|Philadelphia]] in what was then the British [[Province of Pennsylvania]].  He was the son of William Biddle, 3rd (1698–1756) and Mary ([[née]] Scull) Biddle (1709–1789). His siblings included: Lydia Biddle, who married William Macfunn; John "Jacky" Biddle, who married Sophia Boone; [[Edward Biddle]], a lawyer, soldier, delegate to the [[Continental Congress]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=B000440/|title=BIDDLE, Edward - Biographical Information|publisher=[[Biographical Directory of the United States Congress]]|website=bioguide.congress.gov|access-date=2016-04-09}}</ref> who married Elizabeth Ross, sister of [[George Ross (delegate)|George Ross]]; Charles Biddle, and [[Nicholas Biddle (naval officer)|Nicholas Biddle]], Revolutionary War Navy captain.<ref name="Biddle1883">{{cite book |last1=Biddle |first1=Charles |last2=Biddle |first2=James S. |title=Autobiography of Charles Biddle, vice-president of the Supreme Executive Council of Pennsylvania. 1745-1821 |date=1883 |publisher=E. Claxton and Company |location=[[Philadelphia, PA]] |url=https://archive.org/details/cu31924028831259 |accessdate=4 June 2018}}</ref>

As a youth, Biddle was a schoolmate and close friend of [[Mathias Aspden]] and [[Founding Father of the United States|Founding Father]] [[Benjamin Rush]].<ref name="Rush1981">{{cite book |last1=Rush |first1=Benjamin |title=Benjamin Rush's Lectures on the Mind |date=1981 |publisher=[[American Philosophical Society]] |isbn=9780871691446 |page=129 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Sfpu7mMW9qIC&pg=PA129 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
During the [[American Revolutionary War]], Biddle was a captain in the merchant service and participated in the work around of the British fleet's blockade of American ports.  He volunteered in the Quaker Light Infantry and, in 1778, he served under his brother, Commodore [[Nicholas Biddle (naval officer)|Nicholas Biddle]], aboard the [[USS Randolph (1776)|USS ''Randolph'']].<ref name="DAR1904"/>

===Political career===
Biddle served as [[Supreme Executive Council of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania|Vice-President of Pennsylvania]] from October 10, 1785 until October 31, 1787 (also known as the [[Lieutenant Governor of Pennsylvania]]).<ref name="upenn">{{cite web |title=Charles Biddle (1745-1821) |url=https://www.archives.upenn.edu/people/1700s/biddle_charles.html |website=www.archives.upenn.edu |publisher=[[University of Pennsylvania|University of Pennsylvania University Archives]] |accessdate=4 June 2018}}</ref>  He served under [[John Dickinson]] and [[Benjamin Franklin]] and hosted [[George Washington]].<ref name="Stewart2007">{{cite book |last1=Stewart |first1=David O. |title=The Summer of 1787: The Men Who Invented the Constitution |date=2007 |publisher=[[Simon and Schuster]] |isbn=9781416554042 |page=231 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=NveNQBMwpLEC&pg=PA231 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

During his term, he was an ''ex officio'' trustee of the University of the State of Pennsylvania (now the [[University of Pennsylvania]]). He was a member of the [[Pennsylvania Senate]] from 1810 to 1814.<ref>{{cite web | last = Cox | first = Harold | title = Senate Members C | publisher = Wilkes University | work = [[Wilkes University Election Statistics Project]]| date = | url =http://staffweb.wilkes.edu/harold.cox/legis/SC.html}}</ref> He also was an associate of [[Aaron Burr]], having introduced Burr to his wife [[Theodosia Bartow Prevost|Theodosia]] shortly after the death of her first husband, [[Jacques Marcus Prevost]].<ref name="Abraham2013">{{cite book |last1=Abraham |first1=David |title=Aaron Burr - Adventurer |date=2013 |publisher=AuthorHouse |isbn=9781481713146 |page=7 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=q6f1PiiP_6EC&pg=PA7 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

Although Biddle vacated his seat at Council on October 13, 1787 the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania records that his Vice-Presidential term extended to October 31, the date of the next Presidential and Vice-Presidential elections. Biddle was elected Secretary of the Council on October 23.

==Personal life==
[[File:Thomas Sully - Portrait of Major John Biddle.jpg||right|thumb|Portrait of Biddle's son, [[John Biddle (Michigan politician)|John Biddle]], by [[Thomas Sully]], 1818]]
{{See also|Biddle family}}
On November 24, 1778, he was married to Hannah Shepard (d. 1825), the daughter of merchant Jacob Shepard and Sara (née Lewis) Shepard, in [[Beaufort, North Carolina]].<ref name="Powell2000">{{cite book |last1=Powell |first1=William S. |title=Dictionary of North Carolina Biography: Vol. 5, P-S |date=2000 |publisher=[[Univ of North Carolina Press]] |isbn=9780807867006 |page=328 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=VeSDB5tKDagC&pg=PA328 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>  The Biddle family had a summer home outside of Philadelphia that was furnished sumptuously with English furniture and paintings.<ref name="Abraham2013"/> Together, they were the parents of ten children, including:<ref name="Jordan2004">{{cite book |last1=Jordan |first1=John W. |title=Colonial And Revolutionary Families Of Pennsylvania |date=2004 |publisher=Genealogical Publishing Com |isbn=9780806352398 |pages=167-168 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=arAfWBsvO1gC&pg=PA167 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="Davis1975">{{cite book |last1=Davis |first1=William Watts Hart |title=A Genealogical and Personal History of Bucks County, Pennsylvania |date=1975 |publisher=Genealogical Publishing Com |isbn=9780806306414 |page=159 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=-nEHRwRAwxAC&pg=PA159 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

* Mary Biddle (d. 1854), who married John Gideon Biddle (1793–1826), the fourth son of Clement Biddle, in 1820.<ref name="Glenn1970">{{cite book |last1=Glenn |first1=Thomas Allen |title=Merion in the Welsh Tract: With Sketches of the Townships of Haverford and Radnor. Historical and Genealogical Collections Concerning the Welsh Barony in the Province of Pennsylvania, Settled by the Cymric Quakers in 1682 |date=1970 |publisher=Genealogical Publishing Com |isbn=9780806304298 |page=145 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=fmiqHA3Fe2oC&pg=PA145 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>
* Nicholas Biddle (b. 1779), who died in infancy.<ref name="Jordan2004"/>
* William Shephard Biddle (1781–1835), who married Circe Deroneray. After her death, he married Elizabeth Bordeon Hopkinson, daughter of [[Joseph Hopkinson]].<ref name="Jordan2004"/>
* [[James Biddle (commodore)|James Biddle]] (1783–1848), a commodore with the [[U.S. Navy]] who died unmarried.<ref name="Jordan2004"/>
* Edward Biddle (1784–1800), a midshipman with the U.S. Navy who died at sea.<ref name="Jordan2004"/>
* [[Nicholas Biddle (banker)|Nicholas Biddle]] (1786–1844), president of the [[Second Bank of the United States]] who married Jane Craig.<ref name="Jordan2004"/>
* Charles Biddle Jr. (1787–1836), who married Anna H. Stokes in 1808.<ref name="Jordan2004"/><ref name="DAR1904">{{cite book |last1=[[Daughters of the American Revolution]] |title=Lineage Book - National Society of the Daughters of the American Revolution |date=1904 |publisher=Daughters of the American Revolution |page=101 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=p2sZAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA101 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>
* [[Thomas Biddle]] (1790–1831), a [[War of 1812]] hero who died after a duel with a Missouri Congressman over a perceived insult to his brother Nicholas. He married M. Ann Mulllanphy.<ref name="Jordan2004"/>
* [[John Biddle (Michigan politician)|John Biddle]] (1792–1859), Michigan politician who married Eliza Falconer Bradish.<ref name="Jordan2004"/>
* [[Richard Biddle]] (1796–1847), a [[U.S. Representative]] who married Ann Anderson.<ref name="Jordan2004"/>

Biddle died on April 4, 1821 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.  His widow died almost four years later on January 4, 1825.<ref name="Biddle1895">{{cite book |last1=Biddle |first1=Henry Drinker |title=Notes on the Genealogy of the Biddle Family: Together with Abstracts of Some Early Deeds |date=1895 |publisher=W.S. Fortescue & Company |page=8 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=uGw2AAAAMAAJ&pg=PA8 |accessdate=4 June 2018 |language=en}}</ref>
