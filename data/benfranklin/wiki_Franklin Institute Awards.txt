The '''Franklin Institute Awards''' (or '''Benjamin Franklin Medal''') is a science and engineering award presented since 1824 by the [[Franklin Institute]], of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, US.<ref name=FranklinAwards>{{cite web|title=The Franklin Institute Awards|url=https://www.fi.edu/franklin-institute-awards|website=The Franklin Institute|accessdate=2 December 2015}}</ref> The Franklin Institute Awards comprises the Benjamin Franklin Medals in seven areas of science and engineering, the Bower Awards and Prize for Achievement in Science, and the Bower Award for Business Leadership.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.fi.edu/about-franklin-institute-awards|title=About The Franklin Institute Awards {{!}} The Franklin Institute|website=www.fi.edu|access-date=2016-06-24}}</ref>

== Benjamin Franklin Medals ==
In 1998, the Benjamin Franklin Medals were created by reorganizing all of the endowed medals presented by The Franklin Institute at that time, into a group medals recognizing seven areas of study: Chemistry, Computer and Cognitive Science, Earth and Environmental Science, Electrical Engineering, Life Science, Mechanical Engineering, and Physics. The first Benjamin Franklin Medals were presented in 1998.<ref name=":0" />

Medalists are selected by Committee on Science and the Arts (CS&A) after thorough investigation.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.fi.edu/csa|title=Committee on Science & the Arts |website=The Franklin Institute|access-date=2016-06-24}}</ref>

== Bower Awards ==
The Bower Award and Prize for Achievement in Science and the Bower Award for Business Leadership are the newest awards, established by a $7.5 million bequest from Henry Bower in 1988.<ref name=":0" />  The Annual Bower Prizes are $250,000 USD each.
